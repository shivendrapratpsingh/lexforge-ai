📡 All API Routes

Every backend endpoint, what it does, and who can call it

Method Route What it does Auth Required
POST/api/auth/registerCreates new user account with bcrypt password hashingNo
POST/api/auth/[...nextauth]NextAuth login — verifies password, issues JWT session tokenNo
GET/api/auth/[...nextauth]Session management, OAuth callbacks (NextAuth handler)No
GET/api/draftsReturns all drafts belonging to the logged-in userYes
POST/api/draftsGenerates a new legal document using Groq AI and saves itYes
GET/api/drafts/[id]Fetches a single draft by ID (only if it belongs to user)Yes
PATCH/api/drafts/[id]Updates title, content, or status (draft → finalized)Yes
DELETE/api/drafts/[id]Permanently deletes a draft from databaseYes
GET/api/legal/analyzeSends issue to Groq for AI legal analysis with case citationsYes
GET/api/export/[id]/pdfGenerates and downloads draft as formatted PDF fileYes
GET/api/export/[id]/docxGenerates and downloads draft as Microsoft Word fileYes
GET/api/export/[id]/txtDownloads draft as plain text file with metadata headerYes

📖 How to Use — Step by Step

Complete user journey from opening the app to downloading a document

1
Open the App
Go to http://localhost:3000 in your browser. You'll see the dark LexForge AI landing page with a hero section explaining the product.
The landing page is public — no login required to view it
Click "Get Started Free" or "Sign In to Dashboard"
2
Create an Account
Click "Get Started Free" → fill in your name, email and password (min 8 chars) → click "Create Account". You'll be automatically logged in and redirected to the dashboard.
Password is hashed with bcrypt before storing — never stored in plain text
If you already have an account, click "Sign In" instead
3
Explore the Dashboard
The dashboard shows your document stats (total, finalized, drafts), recent documents, and quick action buttons. The left sidebar has navigation to all sections.
Stats cards show total docs, finalized count, draft count
Recent documents are listed with their type, date, and status
Quick Actions panel lets you jump directly to any feature
4
Generate a Legal Document
Click "✦ New Document" in the sidebar or dashboard. A 2-step wizard appears.
Step 1: Choose document type — Legal Notice, Case Brief, Contract, Petition, or Memorandum
Step 2: Fill in the form fields specific to that document type (parties, subject, facts, etc.)
Click "🤖 Generate with AI" — Groq API sends your details to Llama 3.3 70B
Wait 10–25 seconds — a complete legal document is generated and saved
You're automatically redirected to view the finished document
5
View & Manage Your Document
The document viewer shows the full AI-generated content, document details, referenced case laws, and action buttons at the top right.
↓ PDF — Download as formatted PDF
↓ DOCX — Download as Microsoft Word file
↓ TXT — Download as plain text
✓ Finalize — Mark document as finalized (locks status)
🗑 Delete — Permanently remove the document
6
Search Case Laws
Navigate to "◎ Legal Research" in the sidebar. The left panel shows all 6 landmark Supreme Court cases.
Type in the search bar — results filter instantly by name, principle, or keywords
Each card shows case name, year, citation, legal principle, and summary
Relevant cases are automatically cited in your generated documents
7
AI Legal Issue Analysis
On the Research page, the right panel has an AI analysis tool. Describe your legal issue and get an AI-powered analysis.
Type your legal issue or scenario in the text area
Click "🤖 Analyze with AI" — Groq analyzes under Indian law
Results include: analysis, relevant laws/sections, key case citations, recommended action
8
Manage All Drafts
Navigate to "◉ My Drafts" to see all your documents as a card grid. Each card shows the document type icon, title, status badge, preview, and date.
Click any card to open the full document viewer
Status badge shows draft (yellow) or finalized (green)
Click "+ New Document" to generate another document
📋 All 5 Document Types

What each type generates and what fields it needs

📋
Legal Notice
Formal written demand sent to a person or organization requiring them to take a specific action or face legal consequences. Commonly used in property disputes, recovery of dues, consumer complaints.
Required Fields
Sender Name · Recipient Name · Recipient Address · Subject · Grievance / Issue · Demanded Action · Response Deadline
⚖️
Case Brief
Structured summary of a legal case presenting the facts, issues, arguments from both sides, and analysis. Used by advocates to prepare arguments and understand precedents.
Required Fields
Case Name · Court · Facts · Legal Issues · Petitioner's Arguments · Respondent's Arguments · Relief Sought
📝
Contract
Legally binding agreement between two or more parties. Includes service agreements, employment contracts, sale agreements, NDA, partnership deeds, etc.
Required Fields
Party A Details · Party B Details · Purpose · Key Terms & Obligations · Payment Terms · Duration · Termination Conditions
🏛️
Petition
Formal application filed before a court or authority seeking relief. Includes writ petitions (HC/SC), civil revision petitions, election petitions, etc.
Required Fields
Petitioner Name · Respondent Name · Court · Jurisdiction Basis · Statement of Facts · Grounds · Prayer/Relief
📄
Memorandum
Internal legal document providing analysis and recommendations on a legal question. Used by law firms, corporate legal teams, and government departments.
Required Fields
To (Recipient) · From (Sender) · Subject · Background/Facts · Legal Question · Applicable Laws · Desired Conclusion
🚀 Deployment to Vercel

How to make the app live on the internet in 4 steps

📦
Step 1
Push to GitHub
Create a GitHub repo and push the lexforge-ai folder
▲
Step 2
Import on Vercel
Go to vercel.com → New Project → Import your GitHub repo
🔑
Step 3
Add Env Variables
Add DATABASE_URL, AUTH_SECRET, NEXTAUTH_URL, GROQ_API_KEY
🌐
Step 4
Deploy!
Click Deploy. Your app gets a live URL like lexforge.vercel.app
⚠️ Important: Change NEXTAUTH_URL to your Vercel URL (e.g., https://lexforge.vercel.app) before deploying, not localhost:3000.
